## Source C-1

**Source Type:** Press coverage (TechCrunch) — *not* native slack.com/blog
**Title:** Slack, Flickr Co-Founder Butterfield's Business Collaboration App, Exits Beta & Goes Freemium
**URL:** https://techcrunch.com/2014/02/12/slack-exits-beta/
**Date:** February 12, 2014
**Provenance:** Verbatim quotes from article; source is TechCrunch, not Slack's own blog

---

- Slack positioned as a collaboration platform that lets teams "port in conversations and links to other work from dozens of other apps" so they can track project progress in one place and "reduce email overload." [verbatim]
- Stewart Butterfield described Slack as helping the working world emerge from "email bankruptcy," explicitly framing overflowing inboxes and fragmented tools as the core problem. [verbatim]
- Teams using Slack during beta saw usage from "every single team member every day," suggesting the product quickly became a central work hub when adopted. [verbatim]
- Slack integrated with Dropbox, Google Docs, GitHub, and Asana to consolidate "ever-growing flows of data," emphasizing the pain of having information scattered across many systems before Slack. [verbatim]

## Source C-2

**Source Type:** Press coverage (Business Insider) — *not* native slack.com/blog
**Title:** Slack App Growing Like Crazy
**URL:** https://www.businessinsider.com/slack-app-growing-like-crazy-2014-8
**Date:** August 19, 2014
**Provenance:** Verbatim quotes from article

---

- Stewart Butterfield said Slack addressed "a problem that's been under everyone's noses" and claimed "everyone will be using" a centralized internal communication platform within 10 years. [verbatim]
- He described Slack as the result of slowly adding features to an internal messaging system and then realizing "this is a great way for people to work," highlighting product emergence from lived workflow pain. [verbatim]
- Slack had no advertising or formal marketing early on; adoption driven by high-profile companies including BuzzFeed, Dow Jones, eBay, and HBO, indicating product resonance across sectors. [verbatim]

## Source C-3

**Source Type:** Third-party case study (Savvycom Software) — secondary source, describes 2013–2016 period
**Title:** HR Case Study: How Slack Becomes A Place For Optimal & Fun Work
**URL:** https://savvycomsoftware.com/blog/slack-case-study/
**Date:** Published February 25, 2025 (retrospective; covers 2013–2016)
**Provenance:** Paraphrased by user

---

- Preview release in 2013 used invite-only model; 8,000 people requested access on day one, exceeding 15,000 within a week — indicating intense demand from teams experiencing communication friction.
- **Key activation threshold:** When teams crossed ~2,000 messages sent, retention "skyrocketed" — behavioral signal where Slack became deeply embedded in day-to-day work.
- Public launch in 2014 followed by aggressive growth driven by freemium model designed to expose as many people as possible to the product.
- Slackbot originated from Butterfield's need to capture personal notes in a channel — small workflow pain inspired product feature aimed at reducing friction and cognitive load.
