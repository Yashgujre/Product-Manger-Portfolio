## Source B-1

**Source Type:** S-1 Registration Statement (direct listing)
**Title:** Slack Technologies, Inc. – Registration Statement on Form S-1
**Date:** April 26, 2019
**Provenance:** Paraphrased by user from SEC filing

---

- Revenue: ~$12M (FY2014) → ~$105M (FY2015) → ~$201M (FY2016) → ~$400M (FY2017); rapid growth in subscription-based recurring revenue.
- DAU: >600,000 (2014) → >2M (end of 2015) → ~5M (late 2017); strong user engagement expansion pre-IPO.
- Paid users: >600,000 paid users by late 2015; >1M paid seats by 2016; supported by freemium-to-paid conversion model.
- Total paying customers: >88,000 at time of S-1 filing; growth from thousands of paying teams in 2014–2015, indicating significant SMB penetration during window.
- Net Dollar Retention Rate (NRR): ~143% — Slack generated $1.43 in recurring revenue for every $1.00 from the same customer cohort one year prior; strong expansion within existing accounts.
- Expansion mechanism: NRR driven by both seat growth and plan upgrades (free → Standard → Plus tiers), contributing materially to the retention figure.
- Customer mix: Ranged from small organizations to large enterprises; substantial share of early growth came from small teams adopting bottom-up, later expanding to departmental and enterprise-wide deployments.
- TAM: Tens of billions of dollars annually; based on global knowledge workers and enterprise SaaS spend (exact figure not specified in user submission — flagged as gap, see 99_gap_log.md).
- CAC/Payback: Low direct sales and marketing in early years; heavy reliance on organic, word-of-mouth adoption and rapid seat expansion within teams, yielding efficient customer acquisition profile (no precise payback period figure provided — flagged as gap).
- Strategic positioning: Management characterized Slack as a "system of record" for organizational communication — once embedded, difficult to displace; reinforced retention and expansion economics through 2014–2017.
