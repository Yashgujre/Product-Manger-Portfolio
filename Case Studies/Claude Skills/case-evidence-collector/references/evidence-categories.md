# Evidence Categories — Detailed Specifications

## Category A: Earnings Calls / Transcripts

**What qualifies:**
- Quarterly earnings call transcripts (CEO/CFO commentary)
- Shareholder letters accompanying earnings
- Annual meeting transcripts

**Quality indicators:**
- Direct quotes from executives about growth drivers, customer adoption, or strategic priorities
- Specific metrics mentioned (revenue growth, user counts, retention rates)
- Commentary on competitive dynamics or market positioning

**Minimum requirements:**
- Conservative: 5 bullets from 1-2 transcripts
- Balanced: 10 bullets from 2-3 transcripts
- Exhaustive: 15+ bullets from 3-5 transcripts

**Readiness pillar connections:**
- Economic Anchor (revenue model, KPIs, growth drivers)
- Timing / "Why Now" (strategic inflections, market shifts)
- Competition (competitor mentions)
- Segment Clarity (enterprise vs SMB commentary)

---

## Category B: Investor Letters / S-1

**What qualifies:**
- SEC S-1 filing (IPO prospectus)
- Annual shareholder letters
- Investor presentations / pitch decks (if public)
- SEC 10-K risk factors and business description

**Quality indicators:**
- Market size and TAM figures
- Revenue model description
- Risk factors that reveal friction points
- Customer concentration data
- Growth strategy articulation

**Minimum requirements:**
- Conservative: 5 bullets from 1 document
- Balanced: 10 bullets from 1-2 documents
- Exhaustive: 15+ bullets from 2-3 documents

**Readiness pillar connections:**
- Economic Anchor (revenue model, unit economics)
- Benchmarks (TAM, market size, financial metrics)
- Segment Clarity (target customer description)
- Competition (competitive landscape section)

---

## Category C: Product Blogs (Problem Framing)

**What qualifies:**
- Official company blog posts about product vision or strategy
- Engineering blogs explaining technical decisions
- Product update posts that frame the problem being solved
- Founder/CEO thought leadership posts

**Quality indicators:**
- Explicit problem statements ("Customers struggled with...")
- Before/after narratives
- User workflow descriptions
- Technical constraint discussions

**Minimum requirements:**
- Conservative: 3 bullets from 1-2 posts
- Balanced: 7 bullets from 2-4 posts
- Exhaustive: 12+ bullets from 4-6 posts

**Readiness pillar connections:**
- Friction Evidence (problem framing, user pain)
- Timing / "Why Now" (why the product exists at this moment)
- Segment Clarity (who the product is for)

---

## Category D: Launch Announcements / Press Releases

**What qualifies:**
- Official press releases (product launches, funding rounds, partnerships)
- Major feature announcement posts
- Media coverage of launches (TechCrunch, Wired, etc.)
- Conference keynote announcements

**Quality indicators:**
- Launch timing and market context
- Stated value propositions
- Partnership signals (ecosystem positioning)
- Funding amounts and investor names (validation signal)

**Minimum requirements:**
- Conservative: 3 bullets from 1-2 releases
- Balanced: 5 bullets from 2-3 releases
- Exhaustive: 10+ bullets from 4-6 releases

**Readiness pillar connections:**
- Timing / "Why Now" (launch context, market conditions)
- Competition (positioning statements)
- Economic Anchor (funding, partnerships)

---

## Category E: Review Themes (G2 / Capterra / Aggregated)

**What qualifies:**
- G2 or Capterra review summaries
- App store review themes
- User-aggregated review themes (user's own summary is acceptable)
- Customer satisfaction survey results (NPS, CSAT)

**Quality indicators:**
- Patterns across multiple reviews, not single anecdotes
- Specific feature complaints or praise
- Comparison to alternatives mentioned by reviewers
- Adoption friction themes

**Minimum requirements:**
- Conservative: Top 3 praise + top 3 complaint themes
- Balanced: Top 5 praise + top 5 complaints + 3 adoption blockers
- Exhaustive: Top 7 praise + top 7 complaints + 5 adoption blockers + segment differentiation

**Readiness pillar connections:**
- Friction Evidence (complaint themes, adoption blockers)
- Competition (alternative comparisons in reviews)
- Segment Clarity (enterprise vs SMB differentiation in reviews)

---

## Category F: Industry Benchmarks (Numeric)

**What qualifies:**
- Industry reports with numeric data (Gartner, Forrester, IDC, McKinsey)
- Public benchmark studies
- Academic research with relevant metrics
- Company-published benchmark data
- Market size estimates from credible sources

**Quality indicators:**
- Specific numbers with units and time periods
- Source credibility (analyst firm, academic, official report)
- Relevance to the product's domain
- Recency (within or near the time window)

**Minimum requirements:**
- Conservative: 3 benchmarks
- Balanced: 5 benchmarks
- Exhaustive: 8+ benchmarks

**Required benchmark types (at least one from each):**
- Productivity or efficiency metric
- Adoption, retention, or conversion metric
- Cost or economic metric

**Readiness pillar connections:**
- Benchmarks (directly)
- Economic Anchor (cost/revenue benchmarks)
- Timing (market growth rate benchmarks)

---

## Category G: Competitive Notes / Market Size Context (Optional)

**What qualifies:**
- Competitive landscape analysis
- Market size reports
- Analyst commentary on competitive dynamics
- Incumbent vs challenger narratives
- Feature comparison matrices

**Quality indicators:**
- Named competitors with specific positioning
- Market share data
- Differentiation points
- Switching cost analysis

**Minimum requirements:**
- Not required, but strengthens readiness if included
- If provided: 3-5 bullets minimum

**Readiness pillar connections:**
- Competition (directly)
- Timing (market evolution context)
- Economic Anchor (market size for revenue potential)
