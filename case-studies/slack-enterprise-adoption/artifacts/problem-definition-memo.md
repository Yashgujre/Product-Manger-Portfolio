## Purpose
Define the enterprise communication problem, affected users, business impact, and success criteria before solution design.

## Business Context
During Slack's early market-entry window, enterprise communication was dominated by email-centric workflows and fragmented collaboration tools. The observed pattern was high coordination cost, low transparency, and weak retrieval of historical context.

## Problem Statement
Enterprise teams lose execution speed and decision quality because communication context is fragmented across email, chat, files, and line-of-business tools. This fragmentation creates measurable productivity loss and delays cross-functional outcomes.

## Quantified Evidence
- Email remains a dominant workflow channel in enterprise environments (80%+ incumbent share in report context).
- Knowledge workers spend significant time on communication overhead and information retrieval.
- Team communication artifacts are spread across multiple systems, reducing discoverability and continuity.

## Primary Users
- Core contributors: PMs, engineers, designers, and operators coordinating daily execution.
- Team managers: accountable for delivery reliability and organizational communication quality.
- Enterprise functions: IT, security, legal, and compliance teams managing scale controls.

## Jobs to be Done
- Keep me informed without overwhelming me.
- Help me find prior decisions and context quickly.
- Reduce switching between systems to complete common workflows.
- Enable cross-team collaboration without losing governance.

## Constraints
- Enterprise-grade security and compliance requirements.
- Global deployment with data-residency constraints.
- Variable digital maturity across teams and regions.

## Risks if Unresolved
- Slower project delivery and duplicated work.
- Higher onboarding time for new employees.
- Reduced confidence in internal communication systems.
- Higher total cost from tool sprawl and process friction.

## Success Criteria
- Faster activation of new teams into recurring collaboration behavior.
- Reduction in communication overhead and context-switching burden.
- Higher discoverability of historical decisions and files.
- Stronger expansion economics through retention and contract growth.

## Out of Scope
- Replacing all enterprise systems of record.
- Solving every workflow natively in one product.
- Building region-specific process variants before global baseline maturity.
