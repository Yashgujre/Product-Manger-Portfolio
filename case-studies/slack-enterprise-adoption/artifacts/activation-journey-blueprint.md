## Objective
Move newly created workspaces to sustained collaborative behavior and early expansion signals.

## Journey Stages
1. Workspace creation.
2. Team invite and role seeding.
3. Channel structure setup.
4. Integration connection.
5. Habit loop formation (daily usage).
6. Activation milestone crossing (2,000 messages).

## Stage-Level Design Requirements
### 1) Workspace Creation
- Time budget: <60 seconds for baseline setup.
- Minimize required form fields.
- Confirm first step completion with immediate call-to-action.

### 2) Team Invite
- Encourage invitation of at least 2-3 collaborators.
- Support invite via email and directory sync paths.
- Nudge toward role diversity in invited cohort.

### 3) Channel Structure
- Provide templates: project, decision-log, daily-standup, incident.
- Enforce naming conventions for discoverability.
- Include onboarding hints for channel purpose.

### 4) Integrations
- Prioritize top workflow systems by segment.
- Show value preview before full configuration.
- Capture integration success/failure telemetry.

### 5) Habit Formation
- Notification defaults tuned for signal over noise.
- Reinforce interactions (reply, reaction, thread) that reduce clutter.
- Encourage searchable context usage.

### 6) Activation Milestone
- Flag workspace state at 2,000 messages.
- Trigger expansion prompts for adjacent teams.
- Surface admin readiness and governance recommendations.

## Friction Map
- Drop-off at invite stage.
- Confusion in channel taxonomy.
- Integration auth failures.
- Notification overload and muting behavior.

## Design QA Checklist
- Every stage has measurable completion events.
- Empty states are action-oriented.
- Accessibility checks for key onboarding screens.
- Error states include recovery guidance.
