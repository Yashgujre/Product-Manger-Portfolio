## Participants

## Interview Objectives
- Validate pain severity and workflow bottlenecks.
- Identify enterprise adoption blockers.
- Capture success criteria by function.

## Key Findings
### Engineering and Product
- Communication context is scattered, causing repeated clarification loops.
- Channel-level transparency helps reduce duplicate work across teams.

### IT and Security
- Procurement risk centers on identity, auditability, and data controls.
- Centralized admin visibility is mandatory before broad rollout.

### Support and Operations
- Search quality is a primary determinant of trust and self-service.
- Notification overload directly correlates with disengagement behavior.

### Sales Engineering
- Existing team adoption significantly improves enterprise deal velocity.
- Usage evidence is the strongest proof artifact in procurement conversations.

## Decision Implications
- Prioritize activation and search quality early.
- Maintain a dedicated enterprise readiness track in parallel with growth experiments.
- Treat governance and analytics as first-class release requirements.
